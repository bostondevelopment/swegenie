## What this role actually is

Solutions Architect (Vendor-Side) means you're employed by the product company (AWS, Salesforce, GCP, and similar) and your job is making that company's product look — and actually be — right for a prospect or client, across bigger and more strategic accounts than a typical Sales Engineer usually touches. This isn't "SE with a fancier title": you own multi-year transformation narratives, executive relationships, and future-state architecture, not single-product demos. Note the naming trap — some vendors use "Solutions Engineer" as a synonym for this role, others use it for Sales Engineer, and it's a different thing again from a post-sale Customer Solutions Engineer. Title alone won't tell you which one a posting means; scope and account size will.

## A day in this role

Expect discovery workshops and value-stream assessments that define scope collaboratively with the client rather than starting from a fixed spec. You'll lead Proof-of-Concept or Proof-of-Value work directly against a prospect's real data and infrastructure — hands-on, not slideware — and produce the architecture diagrams, TCO analyses, and migration plans that back a large deal. At AWS-class vendors this role is explicitly described as "the technical glue" between support, service, and sales teams, which means real cross-team coordination alongside client meetings, plus periodic travel for onsite sessions and migration work.

## Comp structure

Vendor-side SA comp includes sales-KPI-linked variable pay — bookings, expansion, adoption, win rate are named success metrics — but the split is usually more base-weighted than a pure SE's, not commission-driven in the same aggressive way. At public cloud vendors, senior SAs typically run $165K-$210K base plus roughly 15% target bonus plus RSUs, for total comp in the $250K-$340K range; Levels.fyi shows Amazon Solutions Architect spanning $167K (L4) to $432K+ (L7) with median total comp around $279K, and Salesforce Solution Architect running $150K (Associate) to $284K+ (Principal). The through-line: real attainment-linked variable exposure, but softer than SE, and no formal utilization target the way consulting-side SA has.

## How to test this cheaply

Ask to sit in on (or help prep for) a discovery workshop or executive-level solution review at your current company, even informally, and notice whether translating technical tradeoffs for a non-technical sponsor feels like a natural extension of your engineering thinking or like a separate, draining skill. Separately, try building a TCO or migration-plan document for a real (even hypothetical) customer scenario using a vendor's public architecture framework — the artifact-production side of this role is testable without needing the job title first.
