## What this role actually is

Customer Success / Customer Solutions Engineer is the proactive, named-account-embedded technical advisor role — the deliberate opposite pole from Customer Support Engineer, not a friendlier label for the same job. Where Support works an inbound ticket queue against SLA targets, a CSE owns a book of accounts through onboarding, adoption, and ongoing technical health, running workshops and building enablement plans rather than triaging tickets. It still requires real product depth (every posting reviewed requires hands-on fluency sufficient to run technical workshops and resolve non-trivial implementation issues) — the difference from Support is *when* that depth gets applied: proactive design work on a scheduled cadence, not reactive debugging against a clock.

## A day in this role

Your day runs on a scheduled cadence — recurring account check-ins, quarterly business reviews alongside a Customer Success Manager, and project milestones — not an inbound queue or on-call rotation. You might spend a morning designing a custom adoption plan for one account, an afternoon running a technical workshop to unblock a stalled integration, and log usage/health signals to flag risk to your CSM partner before it becomes a renewal conversation. At companies like Databricks, CSEs act as "quarterbacks" — curating recommendations and coordinating specialist Solutions Architects for the deepest technical work rather than doing all of it themselves. You'll get pulled into a genuinely hard technical issue for one of your accounts sometimes, but that's the exception, not the operating mode.

## Comp structure

Base-heavy, with a modest annual bonus — not sales-style commission, and (unlike a CSM) you're rarely comped directly on renewal or expansion even though your technical health signal is a real leading indicator partner teams rely on. Direct CSE-labeled comp data is thinner than for Support Engineer roles; the closest corroborating bands (Datadog's broader "Customer Success" band at roughly $79K-$113K total comp, and general market positioning "competitive with mid-level SWE at the same company") suggest a structure similar to Support Engineering's base+bonus pattern, just without a clean multi-level published band of its own.

## How to test this cheaply

Volunteer to run one customer-facing onboarding or enablement session at your current company (even informally, for an internal "customer" like another team adopting your service) and see whether designing the workshop and following up over weeks feels satisfying or like unwanted relationship-management overhead. Separately, shadow a CSE or CSM for a quarterly business review — the qualitative "how's this account really doing" conversation, with no scorecard to hide behind, is the clearest test of whether ambiguous success criteria energize or frustrate you.
