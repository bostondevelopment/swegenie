## What this role actually is

Solutions Architect (Consulting-Side) is the systems-integrator/consultancy version of this title — Accenture, Deloitte, Slalom, Capgemini, and similar — where you design across many vendors' products for a given client engagement rather than going deep on one vendor's stack. That's the real dividing line from the vendor-side version of this same title: a consulting SA is paid to recommend the best solution across the client's mixed, changing environment, even when that means recommending against your own firm's practice offering. You typically split time across pre-sale/proposal work and delivery oversight for a portfolio of concurrent client engagements, not one long embed — and while the role does carry real project-management weight, postings (Thoughtworks among them) explicitly expect you to keep coding alongside developers, not just produce diagrams.

## A day in this role

Your day centers on solutioning workshops, architectural reviews, and discovery sessions that translate a client's often-competing priorities (cost, flexibility, service quality) into a concrete value proposition and solution blueprint. You'll produce sequence diagrams, integration models, and level-of-effort estimates that feed an RFP or a signed SOW, and act as the alignment point between the bid team and the client through both proposal and delivery phases. Expect real cross-vendor/cross-platform integration work — provisioning environments, governing clusters, evaluating security and cost tradeoffs across multiple cloud stacks — plus the ongoing reality of being staffed across more than one client engagement at a time, with utilization tracked closely as your core performance number.

## Comp structure

Per the resolved comp figures for this archetype (Levels.fyi as the primary, level-segmented anchor): Accenture Solution Architects run from Associate at $115K up to Team Leader at $169K, with Senior Solution Architect median total comp around $196K (base ~$181K + modest stock + roughly $11.5K bonus — about 6% of base). This is base-plus-modest-bonus, not commission: the bonus ties to utilization and project margin, not deal-by-deal attainment, and equity is essentially absent below partner/MD level. Treat wider Glassdoor-aggregate ranges (which blend many levels and geographies into one number and run meaningfully higher at the top end) as the overall observed market spread, not a conflicting figure — the Levels.fyi numbers above are the better per-level anchor. Whichever number you're looking at, the structural point holds: this is a utilization-linked bonus, not the deal-linked variable comp of Sales Engineer or vendor-side SA, and not the equity-heavy, no-quota comp of Forward Deployed Engineer.

## How to test this cheaply

Take on (or volunteer for) a cross-team or cross-vendor integration problem at your current job — something that forces you to reason across two unfamiliar platforms or cloud stacks rather than your usual one — and notice whether the context-switching feels energizing or just tiring. Separately, try drafting a solution blueprint or level-of-effort estimate for a hypothetical multi-vendor client problem and see whether you'd rather own that design document or be the one implementing it — that distinction (design-and-handoff versus build-and-run) is the sharpest real test of fit for this archetype.
